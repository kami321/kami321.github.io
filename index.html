One-paragraph Description

I find joy in crafting dynamic and engaging maps using Python's Pandas and Folium libraries. Through thoughtful design decisions, I aim to strike a balance between functionality and visual allure in my Seattle library map creations. Leveraging the power of Pandas, I effortlessly navigate through the intricacies of library data, making each row a canvas for exploration. Folium as my trusted companion, paints a vivid picture of Seattle's public libraries, clustering markers in a clean and informative display. The markers themselves don different hues, responding to the library's dual role as a shelter or library only. To enhance user interaction, I sprinkle tooltips across the map, revealing vital details like the library's name, address, and website. Lastly, the TagFilterButton is seamlessly woven into the fabric of the map, empowering users to filter libraries based on nuanced categories like 'Library Only' or the hybrid 'Library and Shelter.' As I navigate through these design choices, I envision users embarking on a journey of discovery, finding not just information but an immersive and intuitive experience.

Reference:

“Seattle Public Libraries: City of Seattle Open Data Portal.” Seattle Open Data, 18 Feb. 2023, data.seattle.gov/dataset/Seattle-Public-Libraries/k3bi-2a3z.
